To analyse my UI And UX I will be using the How to PDF by Peta Golodberg alongside my own additional research. First let us look into the laws that effect UX according to the How to PDF the first being Von Restorff Effect where multiple similar objects are present, one that differs from the rest is most likely to be remembered. On my website, I utilized this by writing some text in bold for information that I want users to pay attention to especially if there is a large sum of writing such as on my blog pages and my name across the website is in bold and differs from the other texts on the site as well as the headings for the blog pages, the font differs from that of the whole blog post itself. (Godlberg,2022)

Fitts’ Law that the time to obtain a point is a function of the distance and size of the target. This can relate to my footer which is present on all my pages at the bottom and in full capacity of the screen width. Because most of my web pages require my user to scroll down my footer is easy to find and the user will automatically stumble upon it where they can retrieve my email and cell phone number to contact me. (Godlberg,2022)

The next element that I will look at is that of a call to action(CTA) this is when a webpage encourages the user to do something. I can say that my home page is where CTA comes into play because my homepage is more of a bait page that is aesthetically pleasing and makes you want to see more. The moment a user opens my site they are placed on the home page where they have the navigation bar that can navigate them to other pages on my site. The home page, therefore, encourages the user to click on the next web page for more information. (Godlberg,2022)

Eligible and legibility, readability refers to the way in which large pieces of text are arranged on a page, and legibility generally refers to the font and whether the user can make out the letters from each other. Throughout my site, all my text is contained within borders that have padding to give the text space alongside with line-height and they mainly all float to the left and is placed on a solid colour background that is lighter in colour compared to the text to create a contrast and making sure the text is readable. The font is also legible because I made use of Open Sans for mainly the large bodies of text and made the font big enough to differentiate the characters. Where I made use of more than stylistic font, I made sure to make the font big so that it is legible and made sure to use a font that isn’t too difficult to read but still looks aesthetically pleasing. (Godlberg,2022)

Visual weight: throughout my website I make use of symmetrical, asymmetrical, and radial balance where my elements on one side of the page have a similar visual weight to the other. Such as on my about page where I have my Image on one side and my block of text on the other side of the page. The visual weight is not the same but looks similar and they manage to balance each other out without one side looking disturbingly heavier than the other. I use the symmetrical balance on my main blog page where I have all my blog page cards lined up in the centre. (Adobe, 2021)

Balance and alignment: alignment is the placing of elements so that they align, when components in a site align it makes it appear clear, confident, and elegant. Alignment in my website is mainly evident when it comes to my text where they all align to the left. At the start of creating my website had my text aligned in the middle, but many people did not like how it looked and said it was a bit jarring to read the text when it aligns in the middle of the page. (Godlberg,2022) Harmony: I can identify harmony across my whole website, harmony is defined as the visually satisfying effect of combining alike or related elements. My site is very harmonious using the same or similar colour palette on all my pages as well as a simplistic layout that is slightly similar throughout. (Godlberg,2022) My site makes for simple and easy navigation which creates a good user experience as anyone viewing my site doesn’t need to wreck their brain searching for something.

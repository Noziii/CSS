For my external JavaScript, I chose to implement the side scroll gallery which I implemented onto my portfolio page. The reason why I chose to implement the side scroll gallery is to optimize the usage of the whole screen. According to an article on Slider revolution side scroll and horizontal layout is good for presenting image content. (Staff, 2022) And my portfolio is all image-based the large scale of the images makes them more eligible and prevents users from having to squint to see the images. The side scroll gallery is also beneficial in that it provides the best possible use of screen space, and it also provides a smoother flow of representation. The horizontal gallery is also good for secondary information, such as images, horizontal scrolling lets the user see a small sample of the content and click for more. This also helps to save vertical screen space instead of displaying my images on a long vertical page. (Beware Horizontal Scrolling and Mimicking Swipe on Desktop, n.d.)

The horizontal gallery creates a better user experience as it gives a different and more intriguing type of design and choice of layout making it more exciting for the user. The user doesn’t have to scroll down a long stack of images where some might not be visible. The alternative option was to have a static group gallery where I have a collage of images for my portfolio all stacked next to, on top, or beneath each other, all in different sizes but this will make it difficult for users to see the images, a horizontal gallery with overlays this wasn’t too bad but when I had implemented it the images looked clustered, and the page felt somewhat empty. I chose the horizontal gallery because I believe it helps to display the images at their full capacity and makes for good interaction and offers a different interaction from my other pages where the user scrolls vertically to look through the information and they make use of the full desktop screen page. It also has a very simple and clean aesthetic that ties in well with the rest of the website.

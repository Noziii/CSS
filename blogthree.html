Accessibility is a very important component to have implemented in one’s design of a gadget, website, video game, etc. But in most cases is very rarely catered for at all and here I want us to discuss the importance of accessibility first let’s talk about what accessibility is and what it entails. According to research accessibility is the design of products, facilities, vehicles, or environments so they are usable by people with disabilities. (Henry et al., 2014) As many of us have seen implementations put in place to cater to people with permanent or temporary disabilities whether it be a ramp for a wheelchair at the mall, a brail on a restaurant’s menu, or a game that caters to colour blind people. These are all elements put in to make these services and products accessible to everyone. Now, why is accessibility important? Well…. It’s important because it allows people who suffer from disabilities to still be able to access daily products and services. This stops them from being excluded and allows them to lead a normal life without much difficulty. People with certain disabilities also need access to necessities such as being able to use a phone, elevator, public bathroom, and other necessities and having access to the internet, and being able to engage with what is presented in front of them. And this makes for an overall good design. But let’s look at accessibility in websites, there are several ways in which a website can cater to disabled people, the website can make use of certain colours which can be identifiable to people who are colour blind. A designer can try to avoid communicating important information with just the use of colour, or text but rather also use sound, this way the designer is catering to people who are blind and /or deaf. Apart from its being inclusive accessibility promotes usability, prompting more people to use the website because it caters to everyone. And accessibility is apparently in the law which is something I did not know and that is another aspect on its own that makes it so important.(“Web Accessibility,” 2018)

Accessibility is a very important component to have implemented in one’s design of a gadget, website, video game, etc. But in most cases is very rarely catered for at all and here I want us to discuss the importance of accessibility first let’s talk about what accessibility is and what it entails. Accessibility is the design of products, facilities, vehicles, or environments so they are usable by people with disabilities. (Henry et al., 2014) As many of us have seen implementations put in place to cater to people with permanent or temporary disabilities whether it be a ramp for a wheelchair at the mall, a brail on a restaurant’s menu, or a game that caters to colour blind people. These are all elements put in to make these services and products accessible to everyone. Web accessibility however according to an article on the site Web Accessibility Initiative, means that websites, tools, and technologies are designed so that people with disabilities can use them. More specifically, people can perceive, understand, navigate, and interact with the Web and contribute to the Web. Web accessibility encompasses all disabilities that affect access to the Web such as cognitive, physical, speech, visual, auditory, and neurological. But web accessibility to people without disabilities such as people using different devices with smaller screens, people with temporary disabilities, older people with changing abilities due to ageing, etc. (Initiative, n.d.)

Now, why is accessibility important? The web forms as an essential resource in people’s lives whether it is through education, employment, recreation etc. It is important that the web is accessible to provide equal access and opportunity to everyone. The article also states that access to information and communication technologies, including the Web is defined as a basic human right in the United Nations Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities(UN CRPD). Accessibility in all aspects is important as to not exclude people with disabilities and those without. (Initiative, n.d.)

There are several ways in which a website can cater to disabled people, the website can make use of certain colours which can be identifiable to people who are colour blind. A designer can try to avoid communicating important information with just the use of colour, or text but rather also use sound, this way the designer is catering to people who are blind and /or deaf. Apart from its being inclusive accessibility promotes usability, prompting more people to use the website because it caters to everyone. (“Web Accessibility,” 2018) Apart from creating an accessible website, a designer needs to evaluate its accessibility and this is mainly done successfully through human evaluation. (Initiative, n.d.)
